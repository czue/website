About Me

Hi, I'm Cory. I'm an American software developer living in beautiful Cape Town, South Africa.

For the past 10 years I've served as CTO of Dimagi, a social enterprise that specializes in mobile applications for underserved populations. While there I led the 25-person team that builds CommCare, thought a lot about productivity, and wrote a lot of code. I’ve recently become interested in product design, marketing, and solopreneurship [link].

In my personal life I enjoy exploring the nooks and crannies of the mountains around Cape Town, pretending I know how to surf, pretending I am in a band, traveling the world, and taking lots of pictures with my Pixel.

I'm originally from the suburbs of Boston, grew up in a family of MIT nerds, and am recently married to a wonderful colleague of mine.

About the Sabbatical

Towards the end of 2016, as I approached my 10-year anniversary at Dimagi, I found myself in a rut.

I was still deeply invested but no longer feeling deeply excited; working long, stressful hours, but without the corresponding boost of energy and fulfillment that usually came with hard work. At the same time I felt like I’d been doing the same thing so long I was no longer learning. My work was demanding but not challenging. I understood how to be a good (or at least okay) CTO for Dimagi, but what else was I good at?

That was when I knew I needed a break.

I have two primary goals for the sabbatical:

  1. Be happier - primarily achieved by working less, and leading a more balanced and diverse life
  2. Chart a future professional path - primarily achieved by trying new things and assessing the feasibility of entrepreneurship

I'm also planning on regularly publishing my progress. Join me on the adventure!
